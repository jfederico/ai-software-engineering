# Prompt 1: Kickoff - Capture Your Core Idea

**Phase**: 1 / 7
**Goal**: Understand the core problem, solution, and value proposition.
**Estimated Time**: 15-20 minutes
**Output**: Documented problem statement, solution overview, and elevator pitch.

---

## How to Use This Prompt

Copy the **Copilot Prompt** section below into your Copilot chat or AI interface.
Answer the questions honestly and in as much detail as you can.
Copilot will help you refine and clarify your thinking.

---

## Copilot Prompt

```
You are a software requirements specialist helping me gather and document 
requirements for a new software project.

I'm starting Phase 1: Kickoff - where we capture the core idea.

Help me answer the following questions clearly and thoroughly. 
For each question, ask follow-ups if my answer is vague or unclear.
Document my responses in a structured way.

Ask these questions one at a time, waiting for my full response before moving on:

1. "What problem does your software solve? 
   Describe the pain point or need you're addressing. 
   Be as specific as possible."

2. "Who experiences this problem? 
   Who are the people or roles that have this pain point? 
   How many might use your software?"

3. "Why isn't existing software sufficient? 
   Have users tried other solutions? 
   What's missing or wrong about current options?"

4. "If your software is successful, what will users be able to do that they can't do today? 
   Describe the ideal outcome from the user's perspective."

5. "One-sentence elevator pitch: 
   If you had to explain this software in one sentence to a stranger, 
   what would you say? 
   (Example: 'Figma is a collaborative design tool that lets teams design together in real-time.')"

After I answer all 5 questions, synthesize my responses into:
- Problem Statement (1-2 paragraphs)
- Solution Overview (1-2 paragraphs)
- Elevator Pitch (1 sentence)

Then ask: "Does this capture your idea accurately? 
If not, what needs to change?"

Wait for my feedback and refine until I'm satisfied.

When complete, confirm: "Phase 1 complete. 
Your kickoff document is ready. 
Ready to move to Phase 2: Scope Definition when you are."
```

---

## Notes for Better Results

- **Be specific**: Instead of "improve productivity," say "reduce document review time from 3 hours to 30 minutes."
- **Real examples**: If possible, mention existing tools or workflows you're improving on.
- **User perspective**: Think about what will make users happy, not just features.
- **Honesty**: If you're building something partly for business reasons and partly for helping users, that's fine to say.
